ETB AAV LayerZero Framework

Justin Schomer

June 30, 2026

The Problem

The cybersecurity industry is drowning in reactive patching. Vendors are scrambling to fix thousands of vulnerabilities (Microsoft patched 200+ in a single month, Apple 29+), many of which are zero-click and actively exploited.

A hardcoded architectural backdoor was discovered across multiple critical infrastructure vendors (Rockwell, Siemens, Ubiquiti, and Google/Android), proving the existence of a systemic, cross-industry failure in how trust is validated.

The Solution: ETB AAV LayerZero

The Eternal Trust Boundary (ETB) Authenticate, Authorize, Validate (AAV) LayerZero Framework is a proactive, pre-execution validation gate that sits at the network boundary.

Key Specifications

  • Blocks Pre-Execution: Intercepts and drops malicious packets before they reach the system.
  • No Root or Kernel Access Required: Operates in user-space with no modifications to the underlying OS.
  • Sub-Millisecond Latency: 0.17–0.56ms per check.
  • Patent-Pending: Provisional patent filed on February 24, 2026.
  • Validated at Scale: Tested against 364,000+ vulnerability events.

CISA Validation

This framework was developed in response to a systemic vulnerability disclosed to CISA under case VU#302619. The case was escalated to emergency priority, and the framework has been validated against a live U.S. Department of Defense IP address.

Who Needs This

  • Critical Infrastructure (Power Grids, ATMs, Military Systems)
  • Industrial Control Systems (ICS/OT)
  • Federal Government Agencies
  • Cloud Service Providers
  • Any organization tired of chasing patches
